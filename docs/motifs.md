# Motifs

Three recurring devices. Used with restraint — the system is quiet by default.

## The glyph as motif

Beyond the logo, the glyph appears **oversized and cropped** at the edge of a
surface as the single decorative element (section slides, hero bands, poster
takeaway). Rules: one per surface, low prominence, never rotated, never glowing.

## The phase band

A thin (4–16 px) horizontal ramp of the phase colours (magma → void → ocean),
`--pt-grad-phase`. It marks **page and section closings** — the foot of a page,
the base of a title/closing slide, the top and bottom rules of the poster.
Always hot-left. Never as a decorative divider mid-content.

## Hairline structure

Structure is drawn with **1 px lines** (`--pt-line-d` / `--pt-line-l`), not
shadows. Cards are flat with a hairline border; radii stay tight (0–8 px,
`--pt-r-*`). A short **magma tick** marks the active nav item or list bullet.
Elevation/shadows are rare and subtle.

## What to avoid

Gradient hero blobs · glass cards + backdrop blur · big rounded "SaaS" cards ·
emoji bullets · decorative SVG illustrations · drop shadows as the primary
separator · the glyph rotated, glowing, or duplicated across a surface.
