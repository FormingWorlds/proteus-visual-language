01Why it matters

Rocky exoplanets accessible to JWST often lie on close-in orbits where tidal heating is significant. As a long-term energy source for mantle melting and outgassing, tides can replenish lost atmospheres on planets around active M-dwarfs.

The L 98-59 system is the prime testbed: three rocky planets, strong mutual forcing, and fresh JWST spectra.

03Results

  • A novel radiation–tide–rheology feedback self-limits tidal heating to ~100× below previous estimates.

  • Self-limited heating still extends primordial magma oceans to Gyr timescales.

  • L 98-59 b is likely molten today, even without an atmosphere.
