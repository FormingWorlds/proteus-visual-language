Model setup

Three coupled reservoirs, one timestep

Interior (SPIDER) — mixed-phase magma ocean evolution in S–P space; melt fraction, heat flux, and solidification fronts on a staggered grid.
Atmosphere (AGNI) — radiative-convective equilibrium at every timestep, producing OLR and synthetic spectra JWST can test.
Volatiles (CALLIOPE) — thermochemical partitioning of H₂O, CO₂, N₂, and S-species between melt and gas, tracking redox state.

Results · Thermal evolution

Tides extend the magma ocean by two orders of magnitude

Feedback between tidal heating and greenhouse forcing varies the magma-ocean lifetime from ~30 Myr to ~500 Myr, sensitive to interior redox state.

Global radiative-equilibrium epochs commonly arise across this range, lasting up to ~320 Myr.
